歴史

京浜急行は、１８９９に始まりました。日本で、３番目の電車の会社です。１番名は京都（１８９５）、２番目は名古屋（１８９８）。だから東京と関東で京浜急行は１番名のでした。その時、東京から横浜まで開通（かいつう）しました。

東京の「東」は　京浜の「京」です。
横浜の「浜」は　京浜の「浜」です。
京浜の意味は東京と横浜ですから、京浜急行になりました。また短くして、京急とも呼ばれています。

今、京急は東京、横浜、羽田空港、逗子、浦賀と三崎口まで走っています。[全部地図に見せる]

赤い電車

早い

一杯駅があるのに、電車が住宅街を通過するのに、早いです。 初めて建てた時、京急はしかし川崎にありました。ちょっと後で、もっと鉄が南に建てられました。そして北にtramlineと一緒になりました。前にtramlineだったから、たくさん駅があるし電車が住宅街を通過するし、shopping districtもbusiness districtを通過します。それにもかかわらず、早いです。top speedは１２０kphです。これは日本のprivate電車の会社で二番目早いです。 どうしていっぱい駅に止まるのに早いですか？ 急行電車も普通電車もありますから。普通電車が各駅に止まります。急行電車は大きい駅にしかし止まります。さらに京急timetablingはいちばんいいから、電子の乗り換える時、本当に待ちなければなります。

Even though it has a lot of stations and runs through residential areas, its fast. When it was first constructed, Keikyu operated in the Kawasaki area only. Soon afterwards the rail was extended south towards Yokohama and joined north with an existing tramline running between Tokyo and Kawasaki. Because of its origins as a tram line, there are a lot of stations and the track runs close to both residential, business and shopping districts. It is therefore very convenient. Despite this, it is also very fast. The fastest speed is 120kph. This is the second fastest of all private railways in Japan. The reason it can be fast and yet successfully serve so many stations is due to a very efficient system of express and local trains. Local trains stop at every station, faster trains stop at the fewer stations and super fast trains only stop at the major stations. The extreme precision Keikyu timetabling means that you can almost always transfer straight from a local to an express train or from an express train to a local train WITHOUT WAITING.

青い席

車掌

To help passengers change trains efficiently, every Keikyu train has a conductor who stands at the back of the train and keeps up a constant diatribe about the next station, the station after the next station, the local stations which you will have to change for at the next main station, the other lines you will have to change for at the next main station. When you change don’t forget to take your umbrella, keitai, bag and of course, watch your step. This is followed by a quick weather forecast, a status update on the well being of his/her family and all washed down with a heart felt rendition of “My Way”. By which time the train is about to pull into said station so the whole message is reiterated again. He can get away with this due to the extraordinarily clear PA system installed on all Keikyu trains, twice as good as JR trains and obviously half a world apart from British Rail. They also have a very unique way of almost singing station names. Its all very entertaining.

And then there are the drivers...

運転者

Like most Japanese trains there are windows between the first carriage and the driver’s cabin. This is great because you can stand at the front with all the kids and watch the train speeding along the track. Keikyu is even better because not only can you see the track, you can also see the driver and Keikyu drivers are the best thing about Keikyu trains. 83% of Japanese kids living on Miura hanto want to grow up to be Keikyu drivers. Like Keikyu conductors, Keikyu drivers are also kept very busy. Not only do they have to drive the train, but they also have to point to everything they see and say its name. To understand just how funny this is, I have to introduce the white gloves. Keikyu conductors and drivers like most “Japanese at work” wear a uniform. It’s a very smart uniform. Blue blazer, blue strides, white shirt, tie, blue peak cap and finally… white gloves – the essential component.

The “changing drivers” routine is probably the best place to start. The new driver waits on the platform for the incoming train. After the train has pulled into the station, the outgoing driver steps out of the train and stands to attention in front of the incoming driver. They bow and then….. salute each other. Then the incoming driver steps past the outgoing driver and begins his “getting into the train” routine. I mentioned before that he has to point at everything he sees and say its name. So off it goes. Densha yoshi, door yoshi, seki yoshi, handle yoshi, tokei yoshi (all Keikyu drivers carry a Seiko pocket watch which they place neatly in the dashboard before driving). Track yoshi, station yoshi, shingo yoshi. Any not just any old sloppy pointing either, this is military style pointing. With white gloves.
